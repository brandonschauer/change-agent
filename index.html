We're running a program to reduce bycatch by getting small-scale fishers in the Philippines to switch to more selective gear. Most fishers understand the problem — they've seen declining catches themselves. But gear adoption is stalling. Where should we be looking for behavioral leverage?

If comprehension isn't the barrier, the stall is likely happening at one of three points: the perceived cost of switching (upfront financial risk, learning curve, social exposure to peers who haven't switched yet), insufficient social norm signaling from influential community members, or a mismatch between when the decision feels relevant and when your program is present.

The most transferable evidence here comes from energy efficiency and clean cookstove adoption research — sectors where the same pattern appears repeatedly: awareness is high, intent is moderate, adoption is low. A 2021 meta-analysis of cookstove programs in sub-Saharan Africa found that programs combining peer demonstration (seeing a trusted neighbor use the equipment successfully) with time-limited financial de-risking (a 90-day trial period, no upfront cost) increased sustained adoption rates by 34–41% compared to information-only approaches.

For your context, I'd suggest testing two things before scaling: first, identify 3–5 fishers per community who are already willing to try selective gear and make their experience visible — a peer demonstration effect, not a testimonial campaign. Second, explore whether a gear-lending or cost-share model for the first season reduces the perceived risk enough to get past the first use. The behavioral lever isn't knowledge — it's reducing the cost of being the first mover.

Capabilities

What Change Agent does

Three capabilities built for intervention designers — not repurposed from a general-purpose AI.

Cross-sector research

Change Agent draws on behavioral evidence from public health, finance, energy, food systems, and conservation — so you're not limited to what's already been tried in your sector. The most powerful behavioral insights often travel across fields; Change Agent surfaces them before you need to discover them yourself.

Intervention critique

Describe your current intervention design and Change Agent will identify gaps, flag weak behavioral levers, and suggest evidence-based refinements. It's the rigorous peer review your work deserves — available before your pilot begins, not after the results come back.

Design frameworks

Access structured behavioral frameworks and decision tools — from COM-B diagnosis to theory of change validation — that keep your team anchored to evidence at every stage. Built into your workflow, not filed away in a methodology document no one reads.
